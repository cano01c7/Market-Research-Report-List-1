<p><p>Residual Solvent Analysis Instruments encompass techniques like Gas Chromatography (GC) and Gas Chromatography Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS). GC is utilized for separating and quantifying solvents in samples by vaporizing them and passing through a column. GC-MS combines GC's separation capabilities with mass spectrometry for detailed compound identification and quantification, offering higher sensitivity and specificity. Both methods are essential in ensuring compliance with industry standards by detecting trace solvents in pharmaceuticals, food products, and other critical applications.</p></p>
