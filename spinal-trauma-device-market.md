<p><p>The spinal trauma device market is divided into two main categories: internal devices and external devices. Internal devices, such as rods, screws, and plates, are surgically implanted to stabilize the spine and promote healing after injuries. They offer a permanent solution for severe cases. In contrast, external devices, including braces and splints, provide temporary support and immobilization for spinal injuries. They are often used in the recovery phase, allowing for mobility while protecting the affected area during healing.</p></p>
